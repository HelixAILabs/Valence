Valence 1.3 changes how Valence arrives on your machine &mdash; and how it feels the first time you open it.

![Valence 1.3 first run - Private AI that lives on your computer]({{ '/assets/img/firstrun-1-3.png' | relative_url }})

## What's new

- **Pick your install.** A new **slim installer (302.62 MB)** looks at your hardware and downloads only the graphics acceleration your card actually uses &mdash; NVIDIA gets CUDA, AMD and Intel get Vulkan. The classic **all-in-one installer (808.47 MB)** is still right beside it: every backend bundled, fully offline, nothing to fetch after setup. Same app, same privacy &mdash; just choose the download that fits how you work. Each install updates in its own lane, so a slim install is never handed the big one.
- **A reimagined first run.** Setup is now a calm, guided experience: Valence takes one quick look at your hardware (right on your computer &mdash; nothing is sent anywhere), shows you exactly what it found, fetches only what's missing with honest per-item progress, and lands you ready to chat &mdash; with your graphics card already doing the work when you have one.
- **Your AI, always in view.** A small status badge next to the wordmark shows which AI you're using and where it runs &mdash; click it for the full picture, including how to switch.

## Fixes

- Offline with a local model or Ollama selected? Messages now send &mdash; the "you are offline" block only applies to cloud providers.
- Updates are now integrity-checked: the installer's fingerprint is verified before it ever runs.
- Older installs that moved to the new data folder now repair their settings automatically &mdash; conversations, profiles, and parental controls all follow to the right place.
- The first-run window now fits all of its content at every display scaling.
